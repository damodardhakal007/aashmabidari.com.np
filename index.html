About Me

I am currently in the second year of my PhD program in the Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering at Monash University, Australia. My research is dedicated to pioneering plant-based dairy mimics, particularly yoghurt analogues.

With a Master's degree in Food Engineering and Bioprocess Technology from the Asian Institute of Technology (AIT) and over a decade of professional experience, I bring a wealth of knowledge in food chemistry, analysis, and processing. My work involves exploring how probiotic cultures and various plant proteins influence the molecular characterization and sensory attributes of dairy alternatives.

I have previously served as a Research Associate at AIT and have contributed significantly to agricultural research in Nepal, focusing on improving crop performance, shelf-life, and food quality.

Current Research Focus

Design rules of Plant-Based Dairy Mimic (Yoghurt Analogue)

Supervised by A/Prof. Sushil Dhittal, Dr. Tayyaba Younus, and Dr. Ram Bhusal

This thesis explores how different probiotic cultures and plant-based proteins (such as lupin and oat) influence the texture, taste, nutrition, and health benefits of plant-based yoghurt. By studying the interaction between ingredients and microbes during fermentation, we aim to develop sustainable and nutritious alternatives that mimic traditional dairy products.

Molecular Characterization

Probiotic Influence

Rheology & Stability

Sustainable Food Science

Developing consumer-friendly, allergen-reduced plant-based alternatives.
